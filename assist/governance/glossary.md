# Glossary

## CODEXSUN

The SaaS business application platform.

## CODEIT

The personal AI development assistant used for planning, coding, review, debugging, and refactoring.

## ZERO

The business companion assistant inside CODEXSUN for business data, insights, predictions, and actions.

## Tenant

A customer business environment with its own users, settings, features, data, and customizations.

## Industry Pack

A set of modules, settings, workflows, templates, reports, and rules for a specific business industry.

## Module

A bounded app or capability inside CODEXSUN.

## Modular Monolith

One deployable codebase organized into strong internal modules with clear contracts.

## DDD

Domain-Driven Design. A design approach that models software around business domains and language.

## Domain Event

A record that something important happened in the business.

## Queue

A background work system for tasks that can be delayed, retried, or processed outside the request cycle.

## Offline Sync

The process of storing work locally and synchronizing it safely with the server later.

## Feature Flag

A runtime switch that enables or disables a feature.

## Activation

The process of enabling modules, features, or subscriptions for a tenant.

## Shared Kernel

Small set of stable concepts shared between modules.

## Enterprise Tenant

A larger or higher-dependency tenant that may require multi-company, multi-branch, dedicated infrastructure, stronger audit, formal support, and custom backup or release processes.

## Vibe Coding

AI-assisted development flow where the developer moves quickly through clear intent, small changes, context-aware implementation, and verification while respecting CODEXSUN architecture rules.

## Quality Gate

A checkpoint that confirms whether planning, building, review, release, or hotfix work is safe enough to continue.

## Observability

The ability to understand system behavior through logs, metrics, traces, alerts, and support views.

## RTO

Recovery Time Objective. The target time to restore service after failure.

## RPO

Recovery Point Objective. The acceptable amount of data loss measured by time since the last recoverable point.
